More about me

Aside from academic pursuits, I get up to all sorts of random things. On this page, I'll briefly mention some of my interests to give you a more complete picture of who I am.

Among many things, I enjoy...

Public Lands

Idaho is comprised of just over 60% public lands, making it effortless to find a nearby spot to camp or hike. As an Idaho native, I'm proud to say that I'm quite familiar with Idaho's forests and mountains, and I'm thankful to live in such a scenic state.

Binders and Manila Envelopes

During my junior year of high school, I somehow ended up with an unlimited print quota in the library. This inspired a tradition of creating a new binder two or three times a year, dedicated to research articles I've found on various scientific topics, ranging from neuropharmacology in the dreaming brain to studies of optical vortex knots. Today, I have a shelf with 18 binders and a handful of manila envelopes for papers I'm currently reading.

Maps

After realizing I had a small collection of maps growing from various backpacking trips and excursions, a friend and I decided to see how much a few specific topo maps would cost on the USGS website. We ran into a bizarre website glitch that allowed certain maps to be checked out for $0 plus shipping. We ended up with a 40-pound box of maps on our doorstep, having each paid only $9.

Since then, I have worked in the University of Idaho Map Room for a time and amassed nearly 500 maps that I regularly use on random afternoon or weekend trips.

Musical Composition

As the son of two musical virtuosos, I've been spoiled with access to innumerable instruments and a wealth of musical inspiration. Despite several failed attempts at taking piano lessons, I never really learned how to sight-read sheet music. However, I've been improvising and composing since then. I mainly stick to jazz and pseudo-classical piano, but every once in a while, I'll create a spacey-synth track.

Postcards

While in middle school, my friend and I came across a box at an antique shop containing a life-long collection of postcards exchanged between members of one large family. After spending a year piecing together the life story of each correspondent, we decided it was time to begin our own collection of postcards.

Ever since, I've exchanged several hundred postcards with friends and strangers around the world. If you haven't heard of it, you should check out Postcrossing!

Cassettes and iPods
