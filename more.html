More about me

Aside from academic pursuits, I get up to all sorts of random things. On this page, I'll briefly mention some of my interests to give you a better picture of who I am.

Among many things, I enjoy...

Public Lands

With over 60% of Idaho being public land, I spend a fair bit of time exploring the nearby forests and mountains. Being an Idaho native, I'm quite grateful to live in such a scenic state.

Binders and Manila Envelopes

In high school I stumbled upon an unlimited print quota in the library which started a tradition of compiling research articles into binders. What began with a few printed papers has grown into 18 binders and a few manila envelopes covering topics from neuropharmacology to optical vortex knots.

Maps

After discovering a glitch that let me order maps for free, I ended up with a 40-pound box of USGS maps on my doorstep. Since then, I've worked at the University of Idaho Map Room for a time and have collected nearly 500 maps that I regularly use for various trips.

Music

Growing up surrounded by music, I've developed a passion for composing and improvising. I mostly stick to jazz and pseudo-classical piano, but I occasionally dabble in synthesizers for more experimental sounds.
