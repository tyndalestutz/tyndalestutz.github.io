About Me

I am a senior in physics at the University of Idaho, aiming to pursue graduate studies focused on photonics and the applications of optical systems. Currently, I am contributing to research in two separate labs: studying visualization methods of numerical relativity simulation data with Dr. Zach Etienne, and conducting research on Optical Thermodynamics and TIRF Microscopy with Dr. Andreas Vasdekis.

Late BNS Accretion

Since joining Dr. Etienne's group in my freshman year, I have worked with undergraduate colleagues to develop a novel workflow for visualizing gravitational waves produced by binary black hole collisions. As a Research Assistant during the summer of 2023, I focused on building a framework for visualizing GRMHD simulation data. Some sample visualizations are provided to the right. Through this work, I gained proficiency in GitHub, Jupyter Notebook documentation, and Unix, as well as solid experience in Python.

Magnetic Fields of Late BNS

Having joined Dr. Vasdekis' team at the start of my senior year, I am currently researching Optical Thermodynamics and TIRF Microscopy. In addition, I have been exploring Optical Thermodynamics as a side project to examine its application to imaging systems, extending the original work in multimode fiber optic systems. Alongside Dr. Vasdekis and Dr. Ram Kasu, I am working with a colleague to investigate the potential use of non-Gaussian beams in TIRF Microscopy.

Outside of university research, I engage in various personal projects, from designing and 3D printing optomechanical systems to building radio and GPS devices. More details on these projects can be found on the Projects page, and further personal information is available on the More about me page. Additionally, my resume can be downloaded here or on this site on the Resume page.
