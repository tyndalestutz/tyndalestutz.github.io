About Me

I'm a senior in physics at the University of Idaho, preparing for graduate studies in Neutral Atom Quantum Computing at Montana State University. I currently contribute to research in Dr. Andreas Vasdekis' lab, studying TIRF Microscopy and Optical Thermodynamics, and previously worked on visualizizing numerical relativity simulations in Dr. Zach Etienne's' group.

Late BNS Accretion

In Dr. Etienne's group, I helped develop a workflow for visualizing gravitational waves from binary black hole collisions. As a Research Assistant in 2023, I built a framework for visualizing GRMHD simulation data, gaining experience with GitHub, Jupyter, Unix, and Python.

Magnetic Fields of Late BNS

In Dr. Vasdekis' lab, I am exploring the use of non-Gaussian beams in TIRF Microscopy while also researching the application of Optical Thermodynamics to imaging systems.

Outside of research, I design and 3D print optomechanical systems and build radio and GPS devices. More details are available on the Projects page as well as more information about me personally on the More about me page. My CV is also available for download here.
