About Me

I'm a senior in physics at the University of Idaho, preparing for graduate studies in photonics and optical systems. I currently contribute to research in two labs: numerical relativity simulations with Dr. Zach Etienne and Optical Thermodynamics and TIRF Microscopy with Dr. Andreas Vasdekis.

Late BNS Accretion

In Dr. Etienne's group, I helped develop a workflow for visualizing gravitational waves from binary black hole collisions. As a 2023 Research Assistant, I built a framework for visualizing GRMHD simulation data, gaining experience with GitHub, Jupyter, Unix, and Python.

Magnetic Fields of Late BNS

In Dr. Vasdekis' lab, I'm researching Optical Thermodynamics and TIRF Microscopy, exploring the use of non-Gaussian beams in imaging systems.

Outside of research, I design and 3D print optomechanical systems and build radio and GPS devices. More details are available on the Projects page. You can learn more about me on the More about me page. My resume is also available for download here.
